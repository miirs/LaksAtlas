The Silent Archive:
Unmasking the Toll

In the cold depths of the Norwegian fjords, a quiet catastrophe is unfolding. Behind the industry statistics lies a grim reality of systemic mortality and compromised welfare.

Impact in Numbers

Annual Mortality Rate
16.7%

Over 60 million salmon perish annually in Norwegian sea cages before reaching harvest weight.

Economic Displacement
5.2B

NOK estimated value lost to welfare-related deaths, highlighting systemic inefficiency.

Biological Pressure
4x

Increase in infectious salmon anemia (ISA) outbreaks over the last decade across coastal sites.

The Escalation of Loss

Longitudinal data shows a concerning correlation between intensified stocking densities and declining individual immune health. This isn't just a cost of business—it's a biological crisis.

CRITICAL
Primary Vector

Sea Lice & Mechanical Stress

Frequent mechanical delousing treatments cause severe physical trauma and exhaustion, often proving fatal for fish already weakened by parasites.

Pathogenic Spread

Open-net pens allow for the unchecked transfer of diseases like Heart and Skeletal Muscle Inflammation (HSMI) between farmed and wild populations.

Thermal Stress

Rising ocean temperatures exacerbate oxygen depletion in high-density pens, leading to mass asphyxiation events.

The Ethical Failure

Current production scales prioritize volume over individual welfare. Every percentage point in mortality represents millions of sentient lives lost in preventable suffering.
